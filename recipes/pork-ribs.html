DESCRIPTION

Pork Rib Stew with White Rice and Potatoes

you can never go back the same

  • Pair with a fresh green salad or steamed vegetables.
  • A squeeze of lemon over the stew brightens the flavors.
  • Ladle stew into bowls, making sure each portion has ribs, potatoes, and broth.
  • Serve with a side of fluffy white rice.
  • Garnish with fresh parsley.

STEPS

  1. Prepare the pork ribs
  • Season ribs with salt, pepper, paprika, and cumin.
  • Heat oil in a large pot and sear ribs until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  1. Build the stew base
  • In the same pot, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
  • Add tomatoes (or puree) and cook until they break down into a sauce.
  1. Simmer the stew
  • Return ribs to the pot.
  • Add carrots, potatoes, bay leaf, thyme, and stock.
  • Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 45–60 minutes until ribs are tender and potatoes are soft.
  1. Cook the rice
  • Rinse rice until water runs clear.
  • In a separate pot, bring water (or stock) to a boil.
  • Add rice, salt, and butter/oil. Cover and simmer on low until fluffy (about 15–20 minutes).
